Notable Races and Moments

Explore some of Formula 1's most dramatic races, title deciders, and rivalries that shaped the sport's history.

Iconic Championship Races

1. 1976 Japanese Grand Prix – Lauda vs Hunt Championship Climax

Fuji Speedway · Torrential rain · Title decided by 1 point

In treacherous conditions at Fuji, Niki Lauda retired early, prioritizing safety after his near‑fatal Nürburgring crash earlier that season.

James Hunt battled through the spray to take third place, just enough to win the championship by a single point and close out one of F1's most intense rivalries.

Hunt vs Lauda in the rain at Fuji, 1976.

2. 1984 Monaco Grand Prix – Senna’s Rain Masterclass

Circuit de Monaco · Torrential rain · Future legend announced

In a small Toleman car, Ayrton Senna carved through the field on a soaked Monaco track, showcasing car control that eclipsed more established rivals.

The race ended early under controversial circumstances, but Senna’s charge in the wet signalled the arrival of a driver destined to become an all‑time great.

Senna’s breakthrough drive at a soaked Monaco, 1984.

3. 2008 Brazilian Grand Prix – Hamilton’s Final‑Lap Title

Interlagos · Changeable weather · Last‑corner championship decider

Lewis Hamilton needed fifth place to secure his first title while local hero Felipe Massa chased a home‑soil championship in front of Brazilian fans.

In late‑race rain, Hamilton overtook Timo Glock at the final corner on the last lap, taking fifth and snatching the championship by a single point moments after Massa crossed the line.

Hamilton’s last‑corner title win at Interlagos, 2008.

4. 1993 European Grand Prix – Senna’s Donington Opening Lap

Donington Park · Mixed conditions · Legendary first lap

Starting fourth on a damp track, Ayrton Senna overtook three cars on the opening lap, using superior grip and racecraft in changing conditions.

He pulled away from the field and controlled the race from the front, reinforcing his reputation as perhaps the greatest wet‑weather driver in F1 history.

Senna’s iconic Donington charge in changing conditions.

5. 2011 Canadian Grand Prix – Button’s Epic Comeback

Montreal · Record‑length race · Last‑lap victory

Jenson Button endured collisions, penalties, and six pit stops during a four‑hour rain‑affected race that included multiple safety cars and a red flag.

Late on, he pressured Sebastian Vettel into a mistake on the final lap, snatching a stunning victory after charging from last place.

Button’s last‑lap win in a marathon Canadian Grand Prix.

Iconic Rivalries

Senna vs Prost – the rivalry that defined an era.

Senna vs Prost

A decade‑long rivalry that featured controversial collisions at Suzuka, intense qualifying battles, and multiple title showdowns.

Their fight pushed both drivers and teams to new heights and helped define late‑1980s and early‑1990s Formula 1.

Lauda vs Hunt – respect, risk, and rivalry in the 1970s.

Lauda vs Hunt

A clash of personalities and approaches, from Lauda’s meticulous precision to Hunt’s aggressive flair, culminating in the dramatic 1976 title fight.

Their story highlighted questions of risk, courage, and where the limits of safety should be drawn in the sport.

Additional Dramatic Moments

Verstappen’s wheel‑to‑wheel masterclass in Brazil, 2019.

Verstappen in Brazil 2019

Max Verstappen delivered a relentless drive at Interlagos, overtaking key rivals on track and managing safety‑car restarts with confidence.

His performance showcased racecraft, composure, and decisive overtakes in one of his standout early career wins.

Räikkönen’s comeback title at Interlagos, 2007.

Räikkönen’s 2007 Comeback

Kimi Räikkönen overturned a substantial points deficit with a late‑season charge, winning the final race in Brazil to claim the 2007 championship.

The tight points spread between Ferrari and McLaren added extra tension to a title fight decided only at the very end.

Lauda’s courageous return weeks after his accident.

Lauda’s 1976 Comeback

After suffering severe burns at the Nürburgring, Lauda returned to the cockpit just six weeks later, still heavily bandaged.

His decision to race again so quickly became one of the sport’s most powerful examples of courage and determination.
